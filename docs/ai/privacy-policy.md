# Privacy Policy

Every application or website that collects personal data must provide a clear privacy policy.

This file is a development checklist, not final legal advice. The final policy must match the real product and should be reviewed for the countries where the product operates.

## Required information

- Product and company name.
- Effective date and last updated date.
- Contact email or privacy contact.
- Personal data collected.
- How the data is collected.
- Why each type of data is used.
- Cookies, analytics, advertising, and tracking tools used.
- Service providers and third parties that receive data.
- How long data is kept.
- How data is protected.
- Whether data is transferred to other countries.
- User rights, including access, correction, deletion, and consent withdrawal where applicable.
- How users can make a privacy request.
- Rules for children and minimum user age.
- How policy changes will be communicated.

## Implementation rules

- The policy must describe the application's real behavior.
- Do not claim that no data is collected if logs, analytics, cookies, forms, authentication, payments, or error tools collect data.
- Collect only data needed for a clear purpose.
- Do not use personal data for a new purpose without updating the policy and obtaining consent when required.
- Add a visible privacy policy link in the application or website.
- Obtain cookie consent before optional tracking where required.
- Provide a working method for privacy and deletion requests.
- Review the policy when data collection, vendors, or product behavior changes.

## Extra review

Get legal review when the product handles:

- Children.
- Health, financial, biometric, location, or other sensitive data.
- Payments or subscriptions.
- Advertising or cross-site tracking.
- Users from multiple countries or states.
- AI training using user content or personal data.
