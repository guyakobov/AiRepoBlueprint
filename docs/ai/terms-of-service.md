# Terms of Service

Every public application or website should provide terms that explain the rules between the service owner and its users.

This file is a development checklist, not final legal advice. The final terms must match the real product and should be reviewed for the countries where the product operates.

## Required information

- Product and company name.
- Effective date and last updated date.
- Acceptance of the terms.
- Minimum age and user eligibility.
- Account registration and security responsibilities.
- Acceptable and prohibited use.
- Ownership of the product, code, content, and trademarks.
- Rules and licenses for user-provided content.
- Third-party services and links.
- Prices, billing, renewals, cancellations, and refunds when applicable.
- Service availability and changes.
- Account suspension and termination.
- Warranty disclaimers where legally allowed.
- Limits of liability where legally allowed.
- Governing law and dispute process.
- How changes to the terms will be communicated.
- Contact information.

## Implementation rules

- The terms must describe the application's real behavior.
- Do not include payment, subscription, refund, or user-content clauses that do not apply.
- Show the terms before account creation or payment when acceptance is required.
- Record the accepted terms version and acceptance date when needed.
- Add a visible terms link in the application or website.
- Do not use the terms to promise features, uptime, security, or support that the product cannot provide.
- Review the terms when pricing, account rules, user content, or service behavior changes.

## Extra review

Get legal review when the product includes:

- Payments, subscriptions, marketplaces, or refunds.
- User-generated content.
- Professional, financial, health, or legal information.
- Children or schools.
- Contests, promotions, or rewards.
- AI-generated content or decisions.
- Users from multiple countries or states.
